Commercial wildfire protection is no longer optional. If you own or manage commercial property, rental units, or multi-building facilities in fire-prone areas of the western United States, wildfire risk isn't an abstract concern. It's a line item that affects your bottom line every single year, whether a fire reaches your property or not.

Rising insurance premiums, potential liability exposure, tenant concerns, business interruption costs, and the possibility of catastrophic loss are all part of the calculation for commercial property owners in the wildland-urban interface. And unlike a homeowner who is primarily protecting their own family, you're often responsible for protecting the investments, livelihoods, and safety of multiple tenants, employees, and stakeholders.

The scale and complexity of commercial wildfire risk demand a different approach than residential protection. Effective commercial wildfire protection addresses the same underlying vulnerability, just at a larger scale.

## The Same Threat, Amplified

Around 90% of structures lost during wildfire events are destroyed by ember attack, burning fragments carried by wind, sometimes over 12 miles ahead of the main fire front. For commercial properties, the exposure to ember attack is often greater than for a single-family home.

Larger roof areas mean more surface for embers to land. Complex roof geometries (common in commercial and multi-unit buildings) create more pockets and crevices where embers can lodge. HVAC systems, vents, and mechanical equipment on rooftops create additional entry points. Exterior features like loading docks, covered walkways, and outdoor storage areas add combustible surface area.

Then there's the operational dimension. A commercial property can't be cleared and prepped by a single person with a ladder and a leaf blower. Maintaining fire readiness across a large facility requires systematic, ongoing attention, and even the best maintenance program can't prevent embers from arriving during a fire event.

## What's at Stake Beyond the Building

When a commercial property is damaged or destroyed by wildfire, the losses cascade far beyond the physical structure. There's the direct cost of repair or rebuilding. There's the lost rental income or business revenue during closure. There's potential liability if tenants' property is damaged or if the property was found to have inadequate fire protection. There's the impact on property value and future insurability.

For multi-unit residential landlords, there's also the human dimension: displaced tenants, disrupted lives, and the reputational impact of being the property that burned while others didn't.

And increasingly, there's the insurance question. Commercial property insurance in fire-prone areas of the US is becoming more expensive and more restrictive. Insurers are conducting more granular risk assessments, and properties that can't demonstrate proactive fire mitigation are finding coverage harder to obtain and more costly to maintain.
